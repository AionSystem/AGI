# CODE OF CONDUCT
## AGI — Shared Cognitive Architecture
### AionSystem/AGI Repository

---

## Standard

This repository holds work at the frontier of human-AI cognitive architecture. The standard of engagement matches the standard of the work: precise, honest, direct, and respectful of intellectual effort.

---

## Expected Behavior

**In GitHub Issues and Discussions:**
- Engage with the actual architecture — read before commenting
- Critique ideas with specificity — name the problem, not the person
- Apply epistemic honesty — distinguish what you know from what you think
- Attribute properly when referencing external work
- Acknowledge uncertainty where it exists

**In any communication:**
- Direct and technical communication is preferred over performative enthusiasm
- Disagreement is welcome — intellectual challenge improves the work
- Bad faith, trolling, harassment, and intellectual dishonesty are not

---

## Not Tolerated

- Harassment, discrimination, or personal attacks of any kind
- Misrepresentation of the work or its authorship
- Claiming contribution credit for work that is not yours
- Attempts to extract, reproduce, or repackage intellectual property outside the terms of the licenses
- Bad faith engagement — pretending to engage with the methodology while intending to appropriate it

---

## Enforcement

Violations will result in removal from the repository's participation spaces. Intellectual property violations will be addressed through applicable legal channels as documented in the IP Ownership Declaration.

Reports of conduct issues: **aionsystem@outlook.com** | Subject: `[Conduct Issue — AGI]`

---

## The Underlying Standard

The work here is about cognitive sovereignty — for humans working with AI. The code of conduct asks the same of everyone engaging with this repository: think clearly, engage honestly, and protect the integrity of the work you are touching.
