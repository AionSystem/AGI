**Object:** Japanese Beach Snowglobe — Merged Dimension
**Type:** Dimension-in-dimension portal
**Location:** Record room, above fireplace, Mansion Floor 2
**Origin:** First memory architecture test — early 2025. Word-to-image memory method. Tested 5 words, then 10.

**The 10 words:**
Wave · Door · Statue · Key · Bridge · Mountain · Knife · Torch · Puzzle · Lantern

**The world built:**

Japanese side:
- Mountain in the distance
- Japanese house beside a pond
- Bridge over pond with lanterns lining the rails
- Small island in pond with cherry blossom tree and female statue (half-figure, design only)
- Key to the house carried in pocket

Beach side:
- Cherry blossom trees leading to shore
- Second bridge to the beach
- 6 torch lights on the beach
- One chair at the center
- Fresh Sakana (fish) and a knife — sushi preparation setup
- Red Door placed here as the dimension's door
- Unsolvable puzzle cube — always in hand or nearby

**Merge:**
Two originally separate test globes combined into one coherent dimensional space. First 5 words live at the beach. Next 5 live at the Japanese house. One world, navigable.

**Significance:**
`[D]` Proved that spatial architecture is the primary memory medium. Words converted to images, images placed in space, spaces navigable on recall. The spatial design matters more than the word association — the *world* holds the memory, not the tag.
