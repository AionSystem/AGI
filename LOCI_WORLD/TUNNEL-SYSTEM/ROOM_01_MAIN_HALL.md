## The Door

**Type:** Metal. Bunker door. Not obsidian — this is industrial. Flat, heavy, hinged on the left.  
**Size:** Standard entry scale — person-height, single door.  
**Surface:** Bare metal. No glow. No marking on the door itself.  
**Mechanism:** Opens outward into the corridor.  
**What happens when it opens:** Fog. Dense, low fog pours out at floor level across the corridor threshold the moment the door swings open. Not cold. Not warm. Just present. It dissipates within a few seconds once the door is held open.

---

## The Room

**Shape:** Circular.  
**Size:** 20 feet in circumference. Roughly 6 feet in diameter — intimate, not spacious. A cylinder cut into the wall.  
**Floor:** Same white marble as the corridor. The fog was sitting on it.  
**Walls:** Circular, continuous. No corners. No features. White.  
**Ceiling:** Luminous, same as the corridor. The light is in the ceiling itself.  
**Contents:** Empty.

## Observations

`[?]` Why fog? The corridor has no moisture. The room has the same materials as the corridor. The fog has no identified source. It did not return after the door was opened and held. Whether it regenerates when the door is closed — unknown. Logged for next entry.

`[?]` The room is empty but the fog suggests something was here or something runs through it. An empty room in a deliberately designed corridor does not usually produce fog without reason. Purpose unknown.

`[R]` The circular shape matches Vault Door 2's elevator tube — both circular, different scales. The tunnel system has a preference for circular geometries beyond the flat corridor. Whether this is architectural language or coincidence — `[?]`.
