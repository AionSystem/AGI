## Spatial Report — First Walk
*Reported by Sheldon K. Salmon. March 2026.*

**Floor:** Smooth dark metal. Cool underfoot. No seams.

**Walls:** Curved, seamless. No panels, no features, no visible joins. Matte charcoal gray verging on black. Not reflective, not fully absorbing — dead flat. Swallows edges without drama. The room reads darker than the illumination should allow.

**Air:** Completely still. No movement, no draft, no vibration. Not thin, not heavy. Pressurized exactly to neutral — registers as the absence of pressure differential. `[D]`

**Symmetry:** Perfect radial. Eye slides around the circular wall without catching anywhere. No focal anomaly. Symmetric to the point of unease.

**Pre-crossing sensation:** Held tension, not stillness. A microsecond of coiled suspension — bowstring drawn to exact full draw, not yet released. Not calm. Not empty. **Primed.** `[D]`

**Doors:** One — the bunker door used to enter. No exit on the far side. The wall opposite is identical to every other arc: seamless, featureless, same matte charcoal. No suggestion of continuation. No outline. No seam.

**Scale:** Room diameter ~6.37ft (20ft circumference). A 6ft person standing at center fits — both feet planted, shoulders clear of the wall, heels and toes inside the boundary by ~6-8 inches. Tight enough that the edge feels real and close. Not cramped. Precisely containing.

**First image — unfiltered:**
> *A perfect black circle drawn in heavy pencil on slightly rough gray cardstock. The circle is so exact it looks machined, not hand-drawn. No shading inside or out. Just the line, razor-sharp, enclosing absolute nothing. The cardstock itself is the room. The drawn circle is the boundary.*

Room presents at 1:1 scale — not miniature, not vast. The human body fits inside the line. Both feet. Square. Grounded. `[D]`
