## The Door

**Location:** Far end of Main Hall. 80 meters from the level point.  
**Material:** Obsidian — same as Vault Door 1.  
**Size:** 8 feet wide × 8 feet tall. Larger than Room 01's bunker door.  
**Glow:** Green and yellow. Both colors in the seams — different from Vault Door 1's blue-red. Not dim — active. These colors are not resting. They are running.  
**Mechanism:** Opens outward into the corridor. Heavy. The weight is in the door.

---

## What Is Behind It — The Elevator Tube

**Shape:** Circular. A vertical tube cut straight down.  
**Diameter:** 10 feet × 10 feet.  
**Depth:** Unknown. The shaft continues below the visible range. No bottom visible from the doorway.

**Wall marking:** On the circular wall of the tube, directly visible on opening the door: **ZONE 2** with an arrow pointing down.

**Control panel:**
- Type: Hand-print activated. No keypad. No buttons requiring manual press — the panel reads the hand placed on it.
- Buttons: 8 total. Floor indicators.
- Current state: Only the main floor (current level — Zone 1) is illuminated. The remaining 7 buttons are present but unlit.
- Implication: 8 levels exist in this shaft. 7 of them are not yet accessible. The system knows where you are. It is not ready to take you further.

## Current Status

The door has been opened. The tube has been observed. The panel has been seen. The unlit buttons have been counted.

It is not time to descend. Zone 1 is not yet complete. The shaft waits.

`[D]` 8 buttons. Only 1 lit. 7 levels below the current floor — their depth, their contents, and the distance between them are all unknown.

`[R]` The hand-print panel removes the possibility of accidental descent. You have to place your hand on it deliberately. The system is not passive. It requires an intentional act to move.

`[?]` Whether the buttons unlock sequentially — one level unlocking when the previous has been walked — or whether they unlock through some other mechanism, is unknown. The pattern suggests sequential. The system has not confirmed it.
